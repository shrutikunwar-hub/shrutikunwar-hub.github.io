A 4-district reading of the hybrid rice opportunity in Haryana — from farmgate to boardroom.

The Brief

Quantify the hybrid paddy opportunity across Ambala, Kurukshetra, Yamunanagar, and Kaithal. Translate 300 field interviews and state-level datasets into a district-by-district entry strategy.

Delivered primary research, competitive benchmarking, and a full 4P marketing mix — with recommendations specific enough to act on the following season.

The Method

02 / APPROACH
01

Primary Research

Farmer surveys — 60 per district — using stratified judgmental sampling. Dealer surveys — 15 per district — using cluster analysis across key retail hubs.

02

Secondary Research

Triangulated against Kisan Bhawan Panchkula records, Haryana Government agriculture data, and Indiastat / Agristat national databases.

03

Sampling Design

Stratified farmers by land-holding size — marginal, small, medium, large. Clustered dealers by geography. Random village selection within each stratum.

04

Analytical Framework

Market sizing, adoption curves, competitive mapping, SWOT, channel analysis, and a 4P marketing mix recommendation tailored for each of the four districts.

The Insight

03 / FINDINGS
Key Finding

Ambala was nearly saturated at 64.5% hybridization. Kaithal — the district with the largest paddy acreage at 166,300 hectares — sat at just 7.9%.

The biggest opportunity wasn't where the market was loudest. It was where the market hadn't started yet.

Supporting Data

High yield drove 63–81% of adoption decisions across districts. Lodging was the #1 barrier in every market surveyed.

Company-driven initiatives — demos, field days, influencer networks — drove 62–70% of farmers' shift to hybrids. Market leaders held 60%+ share in each district, creating clear competitive moats to navigate.
