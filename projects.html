Market Assessment of Hybrid Rice in Haryana

Conducted a comprehensive market assessment across 4 districts of Haryana (Ambala, Kurukshetra, Yamunanagar, Kaithal) to develop an entry strategy for hybrid paddy. The research spanned primary surveys with 300 respondents, secondary data analysis, competitive benchmarking, and a full 4P marketing mix recommendation.

Submitted to MANAGE, Hyderabad as part of MBA research (2019).

Research Methodology

Primary Research

Farmer surveys (60/district) using stratified judgmental sampling, plus dealer surveys (15/district) using cluster analysis.

Secondary Research

Data from Kisan Bhawan Panchkula, Haryana Government records, Indiastat/Agristat databases.

Sampling Design

Stratified by farmer size (marginal, small, medium, large). Cluster sampling for dealers. Random village selection.

Analytical Framework

Market sizing, adoption curves, competitive mapping, SWOT, channel analysis, and 4P marketing mix per district.

Key Findings

Market Insights

Ambala had the highest hybridization rate (64.5%) and was nearing saturation, while Kaithal showed just 7.9% with the largest paddy acreage (166,300 ha) — massive untapped potential.

High yield was the primary trigger for adoption across all districts (63-81%), while lodging was the #1 barrier. Company-driven initiatives influenced 62-70% of farmers' shift to hybrids. Market leaders held 60%+ share in each district.
